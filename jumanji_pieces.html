Abstract

Recently we got the board game Jumanji , which came in a cool wooden box, but the pieces included are somewhat disappointing (just standard colored game pieces).

I figured I could do one better by making my own pieces that are closer to the ones in the movie.

The original game pieces. Not very exciting

Modeling

I have a ZIP archive of the Jumanji board and pieces in STL format. Unfortunately, I can't find the original free source online. I believe it was SketchFab or somewhere similar.

Printing & Paint

Using the STL files for the player pieces, I printed them in Hatchbox Wood filament at 0.1mm layer height.

Primer and first coat of black paint applied

Something I wasn't sure about was how the primer & paint would cover for the layering inherent with FDM printers. The answer was hit or miss. Smooth changes in angle look quite good, but certain curved edges and the top-most layers are fairly obvious.

One solution would be to simply do more sanding. I did sand away some imperfections caused by stringing, but each model could've done with a once-over with a heavy grit sandpaper. In any case, not a huge deal, just something to note in the future.

Base coats are done

Results

The finished pieces

I'm overall pretty happy with how they turned out. I learned quite a bit about painting miniatures in general.

Some paint details:
The Alligator Crocodile is a black base with a silver dry-brush.
The Monkey is a black base with a thin green coat.
The Rhino is a white base with a brown wash.
The Elephant is simply a coat of black with touch-ups over it.
